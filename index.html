What to do in Austin

Austin City Limits: Where PBS films their popular Austin City Limits music series.

Stubbs: Outdoor venue with a BBQ restaurant attached. Also home to a gospel brunch every Sunday.

East East 6h St / Hipster 6th: That's what they call 6th street east of I35. They will usually have live music at a variety of bars for a low cover.

Rainey St.: You can jump around to different bars within in a very small area and typically a few will have bands with no cover.

South Congress: Many famous shops, bars and restaurants. A few favorites include Jo's, Continental Club and Guerro's.

The Elephant Room: Very close to the JW with live jazz music.

Threadgills: Restaurant with live bands most weekend nights and a gospel brunch on Sundays

Shady Grove: Restaurant with a stage outside on the walk towards Zilker Park / Barton Springs.

Zilker Park: Home to Austin City Limits Music Festival and many local festivals such as Kite Fest and Trail of Lights.

Barton Springs: Local swimming hole and as classic an Austin establishment as you can find.

Franklin BBQ: World famous BBQ. Go early and wait in line or order ahead and take it home with you.

Fareground: Hipster cafeteria that recently opened in the Wells Fargo building right across the street from the JW.

Cap 10K: Iconic race around Austin taking place the morning after the wedding. If you make it up for this and complete the race you win a prize.

Volstead Lounge: Where it all began. Bar on the East Side where Ashley and Matt met.
